## Omnivore has shut down: what now?

Omnivore was a promising open-source read-it-later app that gained a loyal following for its clean interface, highlights, and integrations with tools like Obsidian and Logseq. In October 2024, its founding team was acquired by ElevenLabs, an AI voice startup, and the hosted service was shut down.

For many readers, Omnivore was not just a tool — it was a philosophy: open source, community-driven and free. If you are looking for something that carries those values forward — and sticks around — here is what to consider.

## Some alternatives

Several services are picking up readers who used Omnivore:

- Readwise Reader is a full reading environment with highlights, notes and AI-assisted features. It is powerful, but at €96 a year it is significantly more expensive, and it is a proprietary, US-based service.
- Instapaper offers a clean reading experience with highlights and notes. Its best features, however, are locked behind an expensive subscription, and its ownership history is uncertain.
- Raindrop.io is more of a visual bookmarking tool than a true reading app. It does have a reader view, but its core focus is organising links and websites.

If what you valued in Omnivore was the open-source ethos, privacy, and actually reading your saved articles — one alternative stands out.

## Meet wallabag.it

wallabag.it is the hosted version of wallabag, an open-source read-it-later app that has been running since 2013. It was built around one idea: **your reading list should belong to you**.

Here is why it makes a strong alternative to Omnivore:

wallabag does not track you, sell your data or show you ads. It is open-source software, run by people who believe in user freedom rather than exploitation.

### Hosted in Europe

All data on wallabag.it is stored on servers located in Europe, protected by strict EU privacy laws and fully GDPR-compliant. A genuine reassurance for anyone wary of US data-handling practices.

### Offline reading

### Tags, annotations and search

Organise your articles with tags, take notes, and find past reads quickly with full-text search.

### Easy migration from Omnivore

wallabag.it includes a built-in import tool that supports Omnivore exports — your articles come across in one step, with their metadata intact.
